June 5, 2023
Smart Family Magazine

“As I was growing up, I was very impatient within myself and I couldn’t really concentrate well on things. Through origami, my patience started growing and my concentration level increased with it. Origami has now become like a form of meditation to me. For instance, whenever I get stressed or frustrated, it is the one thing that calms me down. Normally, every time I make something new, my entire intention behind it is to give it to friends and family as a gift, keeping in mind what they would like. I don’t personally own all of my models at the moment.”

The Kathmandu Post

For him, making origami is like meditating. “When I start folding papers at night after finishing all of my work for the day, all of my stress gets relieved,” says Rajbhandari. “As someone who started origami as a hobby years ago, now it has become a part of me. When I fold papers with a motive of making something, it feels like I am meditating, focusing my whole energy with full discipline, to create something that becomes a medium of my expression.”
